Indian Independence Timeline

1857

First War of Independence

The Sepoy Mutiny marked the first major uprising against British rule, though unsuccessful, it planted seeds of resistance.

1885

Formation of Indian National Congress

The INC was established as a platform for political dialogue and later became the primary vehicle for independence.

1919

Jallianwala Bagh Massacre

British troops fired on unarmed civilians, killing hundreds and galvanizing the independence movement.

1930

Salt March (Dandi March)

Gandhi's 240-mile march to the sea to make salt became a symbol of non-violent resistance.

1942

Quit India Movement

A mass protest demanding an end to British rule, with the slogan 'Do or Die' inspiring millions.

1947

Independence Day

At the stroke of midnight, India became free from British colonial rule after nearly 200 years.
