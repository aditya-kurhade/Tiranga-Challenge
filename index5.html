Independence Milestones üó≥Ô∏è

Formation of Indian National Congress (1885)

Founded by A.O. Hume, the Indian National Congress became the primary platform for India's independence movement, uniting leaders to demand self-governance and foster national consciousness.

Non-Cooperation Movement (1920-1922)

Initiated by Mahatma Gandhi in response to the Jallianwala Bagh massacre, this movement encouraged Indians to boycott British goods, schools, and institutions, promoting swadeshi (self-reliance) and galvanizing widespread participation.

Civil Disobedience Movement (1930)

Launched with Gandhi's Dandi Salt March, this campaign defied British salt laws, sparking nationwide protests. It highlighted non-violent resistance, drawing global attention to India's struggle for freedom.

Quit India Movement (1942)

Launched on August 8, 1942, with Gandhi's "Do or Die" call, this mass movement demanded an immediate end to British rule. Despite severe repression, it unified Indians across regions and intensified the independence struggle.

Indian Independence Act (1947)

Passed by the British Parliament, this act granted independence to India on August 15, 1947, partitioning the subcontinent into India and Pakistan. Jawaharlal Nehru's "Tryst with Destiny" speech marked the historic moment.

Constitution Adoption (1950)

On January 26, 1950, India became a republic with the adoption of its Constitution, drafted under Dr. B.R. Ambedkar‚Äôs leadership. It established India as a sovereign, democratic nation with fundamental rights for all citizens.

National Flag

The Tiranga, adopted on July 22, 1947, features saffron for courage, white for peace, green for prosperity, and the Ashoka Chakra for righteousness. Designed by Pingali Venkayya, it symbolizes India's unity and independence.

Adopted on January 26, 1950, the emblem is derived from the Lion Capital of Ashoka at Sarnath, featuring four lions symbolizing power, courage, pride, and confidence, with the motto "Satyameva Jayate" (Truth Alone Triumphs).
